Getting through the eligibility threshold is one thing. Understanding what Legal Aid Ontario will actually fund once you qualify is another.

The coverage is real and meaningful. It is also narrower than many people expect. This article maps the scope of both duty counsel and certificate coverage so you arrive at any court date knowing exactly what help you can ask for.

  This article provides legal information about Legal Aid Ontario service coverage. It is not legal advice. For advice specific to your situation, contact LAO at 1-800-668-8258 or consult a licensed Ontario lawyer.
</Callout>

## What family duty counsel covers

Family duty counsel is courthouse assistance for people with a court date. The coverage is specific to early and resolution stages of a proceeding.

Duty counsel can help with:

- Case conferences
- Settlement conferences
- First appearances
- Procedural guidance (what to do next, what documents are needed)
- Brief review of documents before a court event
- Explaining your rights and the applicable law in plain language

This assistance is brief and event-specific. Duty counsel is not your lawyer throughout the proceeding. Their role ends when the court event ends.

---

## What family duty counsel does NOT cover

<Callout type="warning" title="Duty counsel limitations">
  Family duty counsel does not cover trials, pre-trial applications, or preliminary motions. It does not cover property division. It does not cover support matters where the client receives government benefits and has assigned their support entitlement to the government. It does not provide assistance at FRO first appearances, refraining motions, or adjournments. It is not available to assist those who already have a lawyer.
</Callout>

The exclusions matter practically. If your case has reached trial or involves a property division claim, duty counsel is not available for those stages. If your primary income-related dispute involves support payments that have been assigned to the government because you receive Ontario Works or ODSP, the support component falls outside duty counsel's scope.

## What a legal aid certificate covers

A certificate funds ongoing representation by a private lawyer for a specified number of hours. For family law, certificates are issued for matters that have legal merit and fall within LAO's covered case types.

Covered family law matters for certificates include:

- Custody and access (now decision-making responsibility and parenting time under the *Divorce Act*)
- Child support and spousal support
- Restraining orders
- Variation applications (changing an existing order)
- Certain property matters, depending on merit and circumstance

Property claims are more complex. Whether a certificate covers a specific property claim depends on the merits of that claim and the circumstances of the case. A certificate is not automatic for every type of property dispute.

## The certificate hour limit

When LAO issues a certificate, it authorizes a specific number of legal aid hours. Those hours are the budget for your representation. When they run out, your lawyer must either stop the funded work or apply to LAO for an extension.

Extensions are not automatic. They require a new merit review and a showing that the additional hours are justified. If no extension is granted and hours are exhausted, you may be without funded representation at a critical stage.

Understanding the hour limit at the start of a case affects how you use legal aid hours strategically. Hours spent on correspondence and procedural filings are hours not available for preparation and hearings.

---

## Legal aid and the other side

Where LAO issues a certificate to one party in a proceeding, LAO will generally issue one to the other party too, if that party is financially eligible and their position has merit. This means legal aid does not automatically create an imbalance between parties. Both sides may have funded representation.

---

## What legal aid is not

Legal aid is not unlimited. It is not a guarantee of winning. It is not a substitute for the work the client must do: gathering documents, providing instructions, attending court.

An LAO certificate funds legal representation up to the authorized hours. The quality of that representation, and the outcome it produces, depends on many factors that legal aid funding alone does not determine.
