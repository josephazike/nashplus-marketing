In some Ontario family law proceedings involving children, a court may decide that an independent voice is needed — someone who can represent the child's interests or bring the child's perspective to the court's attention. That independent voice is the Office of the Children's Lawyer.

Most people navigating a parenting dispute have not heard of it. Many assume they can contact it directly. Neither assumption helps. This article explains what the OCL actually does, when it gets involved, and what its involvement means for a case.

## What the OCL is

The Office of the Children's Lawyer is an independent law office within the Ontario Ministry of the Attorney General. It delivers legal services on behalf of children. Its staff includes lawyers who represent children directly and clinicians — typically social workers — who assess children's circumstances and prepare reports.

The OCL is not a private firm you can retain. It is a public office. Its involvement in a case is initiated by a court, not by a parent.

---

## When courts involve the OCL

A court may request OCL involvement when:

- The parenting dispute is contested and the child's views or circumstances are not adequately before the court
- The parents' accounts differ significantly about what is in the child's best interests
- The child is old enough or mature enough that their views are a significant factor in the best interests analysis
- There are concerns about the child's safety, adjustment, or well-being that require independent investigation

The court makes the referral. The OCL accepts or declines based on its assessment of the file. The OCL is not required to take every case referred to it.

<Callout type="note" title="You cannot request the OCL yourself">
  Parents do not directly request OCL involvement. Either party can ask the court to consider making a referral. The decision to involve the OCL belongs to the court. Once involved, the OCL acts independently — it represents the child's interests, not either parent's position.
</Callout>

---

## What the OCL does when involved

Once the OCL accepts a referral, it assigns either a lawyer, a clinician, or both, depending on what is needed.

**A Children's Lawyer Report** is prepared by an OCL clinician. The clinician interviews the child, each parent, and other relevant people (teachers, extended family, therapists). They observe the child in each household where possible. The report summarizes what the clinician found and may include a recommendation about what arrangement would serve the child's best interests.

**A Voice of the Child Report** is a more focused document. It records the child's expressed views and preferences without the full clinical assessment. It is used when the primary purpose is to bring the child's own perspective before the court.

**Legal representation by an OCL lawyer** means the lawyer advocates for a position consistent with the child's views and preferences. Where the child is old enough to have meaningful views, the OCL lawyer advances those views. Where the child is too young, the lawyer may take a best-interests position instead.

The OCL can provide one or more of these services in the same case.

---

## What an OCL report does — and does not — do

An OCL report is evidence. It is one piece of the case. Courts give it significant weight because of the OCL's independent status and expertise. But the court is not bound by an OCL recommendation.

The OCL does not decide what is in the child's best interests. The court decides. The OCL's role is to provide information the court may not otherwise have — the child's perspective, the child's actual day-to-day circumstances, and an independent professional's assessment of what those circumstances suggest.

## What the OCL does not do

The OCL does not:

- Get involved before a court proceeding has started (with limited exceptions in child protection matters)
- Provide services to parents — it represents children
- Give legal advice to either parent
- Supervise parenting time or transitions
- Make or enforce court orders
- Advise on immigration, education, or mental health matters outside its mandate

If you are in a parenting dispute and are looking for independent support for your own position, the OCL is not that resource. It serves the child.

---

## How long OCL involvement takes

OCL investigations take time. A full Children's Lawyer Report can take several months from referral to delivery. During that time, the case typically continues, and interim arrangements govern where the child lives and how decisions are made.

If you anticipate that OCL involvement may be relevant to your case, factoring in the time it requires is part of realistic planning for the proceeding.
