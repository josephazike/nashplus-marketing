One of the most common misunderstandings about Legal Aid Ontario is that there is a single application process for all services. There is not.

Family duty counsel at the courthouse requires no advance application at all. A legal aid certificate for ongoing representation requires a separate process. Knowing which one applies to your situation determines what you need to do — and when.

<Callout type="legal">
  This article provides legal information about accessing Legal Aid Ontario services. It is not legal advice. For advice specific to your situation, contact LAO directly at 1-800-668-8258 or consult a licensed Ontario lawyer.
</Callout>

## Accessing family duty counsel: no application needed

Family duty counsel is available at Ontario family courthouses. The process is not an application. It is a walk-in service, available on the day of your court appearance.

## Applying for a legal aid certificate

A legal aid certificate authorizes you to retain a private lawyer for ongoing representation. The application is more involved than accessing duty counsel, and there is a separate, lower income threshold.

## What to prepare before you apply

<Checklist
  heading="Documents to have ready for a certificate application"
  items={[
    "Income tax return for the most recent year, with your Notice of Assessment",
    "Recent pay stubs (last 4-6 pay periods for salaried employment; more for variable income)",
    "Bank account statements for the last 3 months (all accounts)",
    "Information about any property you own (home, vehicle, investments)",
    "Details about any debts or liabilities that may affect your net asset position",
    "Your court file number and information about your court dates, if the proceeding has started",
    "The names of the other parties in the proceeding",
    "A brief description of the legal issue and what you are seeking from the court",
  ]}
/>

LAO has a financial eligibility calculator on its website. Using it before calling can help you understand whether you are likely to meet the thresholds before you begin the formal process.

## If your situation is urgent

For urgent matters involving domestic violence, child safety, or custody emergencies, tell LAO when you call. The contact centre at 1-800-668-8258 can direct you to the most relevant service. LAO gives priority to survivors of intimate partner violence and cases involving the safety of a spouse or child.

If you cannot wait for a certificate and have an urgent court date, go to the FLIC at your local courthouse. Duty counsel may be able to assist on the day even if a certificate application is still pending.

---

## Certificate contribution: you may need to contribute

If your income is above the lower income threshold but you still qualify for a certificate, LAO may require a financial contribution toward the cost of your legal representation. The contribution amount depends on your income level. This is different from full cost — you are not paying the lawyer's hourly rate. But it is not entirely free either.

LAO will tell you the contribution amount when the certificate is issued. That is not legal advice. It is a financial arrangement between you and LAO.
