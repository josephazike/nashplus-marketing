<Callout type="warning" title="Immediate danger">
  If you are in immediate danger of harming yourself or others, call 911 or go to your nearest hospital emergency department.
</Callout>

If you are not in immediate danger but are in distress, or if you are not certain what you need, the resources below are available. You do not have to know exactly what kind of help you are looking for to reach out.

Phone numbers on this page were verified in May 2026. Crisis resources and contact details change. If a number does not connect, go to the organization's website for current information.

---

## 9-8-8 Suicide and Crisis Helpline (Canada)

**Call or text: 9-8-8**
Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

This is Canada's national suicide and crisis helpline. It is available in English and French. It is free. You do not have to be in active suicidal crisis to call — the line is for anyone experiencing emotional distress, including people who are uncertain whether what they are feeling is "serious enough."

*Source: 988.ca, May 2026*

## ConnexOntario

**Phone: 1-866-531-2600**
**Text:** CONNEX to 247247
**Web chat:** connexontario.ca
Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, in over 130 languages.

ConnexOntario provides information and referrals to Ontario mental health, addictions, and problem gambling services. If you are not sure what kind of support you need or where to find it in your community, this is a useful first call. Trained specialists help identify what is available and how to access it.

*Source: connexontario.ca, May 2026*

## Assaulted Women's Helpline

**Toll-free phone: 1-866-863-0511**
**TTY (toll-free): 1-866-863-7868**
**Text:** #SAFE (#7233)
Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, in over 200 languages.

This helpline provides counselling, emotional support, information, and referrals to women who have experienced abuse. If you are experiencing intimate partner violence or abuse in the context of a separation, this line is available regardless of where in Ontario you are.

*Source: awhl.org, May 2026*

## Kids Help Phone

**Phone: 1-800-668-6868**
**Text:** CONNECT to 686868
Available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Kids Help Phone serves children, teens, and young adults across Canada. If you are reading this page for yourself and you are a young person, this line is for you. If you are a parent worried about your child, Kids Help Phone can also provide guidance.

*Source: kidshelpphone.ca, May 2026*

## BounceBack Ontario (non-crisis, low-to-moderate distress)

**Phone: 1-866-345-0224**
**Web:** bouncebackontario.ca

BounceBack is a free, OHIP-funded program for adults and youth 15 and older experiencing mild to moderate depression, low mood, stress, or anxiety. It is not a crisis line. It is an access point for people who are struggling but not in acute crisis and who are looking for structured, evidence-based support without a long wait or a large expense.

Self-referral is accepted. A family doctor referral is another pathway in.

*Source: bouncebackontario.ca, May 2026*

## ConnexOntario for all other mental health and addictions help

**Phone: 1-866-531-2600** (same as above)

ConnexOntario is the most useful single number for navigating the Ontario mental health system. If none of the other resources above fits your situation, or if you are looking for ongoing support rather than immediate crisis help, start here.

## If you are worried about someone else

You cannot call a crisis line on someone else's behalf in most cases. What you can do is:

- Be present and listening without judgment
- Ask directly if they are thinking about suicide — asking does not increase risk, and most people find the directness a relief
- Help them find or call a resource if they are willing
- Call 911 if you believe they are in immediate danger

The 9-8-8 line can also provide guidance on how to support someone you are concerned about.

If you are uncertain about the severity of what you or someone else is experiencing, calling any of these lines and describing the situation is a reasonable next step. The people on these lines are trained to help you figure out what kind of support is needed.
