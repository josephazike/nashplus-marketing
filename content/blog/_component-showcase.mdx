## 1. Callout

Four types: `note`, `tip`, `warning`, `legal`.

<Callout type="note">
  Form 13 is used when the proceeding involves support claims only. If property division is also at issue, you need Form 13.1.
</Callout>

<Callout type="tip" title="Practical tip">
  Gather three years of tax returns and notices of assessment before your first court date. Courts regularly adjourn proceedings because financial disclosure is incomplete.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning" title="Deadline">
  Financial statements must be served and filed at least 30 days before a conference or motion at which support will be decided (Family Law Rules, Rule 13(6)).
</Callout>

<Callout type="legal">
  This article provides legal information, not legal advice. The rules described apply to Ontario family proceedings. For advice specific to your situation, consult a licensed Ontario lawyer.
</Callout>
